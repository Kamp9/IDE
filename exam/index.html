Introduction

In the last 20 years more than 3,700 exoplanets have been discovered. Exoplanets are planets which orbit a star outside of our solar system. Most of these planets are found by the Kepler telescope. We visualize the planets discovered by the Kepler telescore, with a focus on potential habitable exoplanets. For a planet to be habitable for humans it needs to have almost the same conditions as our own earth. One of the criterias for this is that the planet is inside the suns habitable zone.
